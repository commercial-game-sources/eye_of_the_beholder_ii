If I understand you, you are interested in:
a) level maps
According to Darkstar (from REWiki) they are AESOP resources numbers 5 to 18.
See the part "Level files" in:
http://rewiki.regengedanken.de/wiki/EYE.RES

b) game scripts
Well, the EOB 3 scripts are not available in source code (only in AESOP documentation there is the content of the script defining START object in the EOB 3 - but I guess it is not much use to you).
The best you can get is bytecode disassembler of scripts from DAESOP. But as any disassembler of a high level language it is not so easy to understand from it what the original program does... It is simpler than with normal assembler (there are symbolic names of runtime functions and imported/exported variables) but still not easy.

So there are three possibilities:
1. Trying to understand the game mechanism by studying the disassembler (not easy)
2. Recreate the game according to how it behaves towards user (without studying how it works internally)
3. Porting the AESOP/32 engine to Amiga (as you know we have C sources for AESOP/32 from John Miles, though the game resources still need some conversions to run in it - I did graphic, but fonts, maybe sound, music must be done as well - I hope to get to it later).

Mirek

Content rights
I've been in contact with Joseph Hewitt, one of the original graphicians providing graphics to the EOB series etc, about content rights. It's a mess....

To make a LONG story short:

EOB was developed by Westwood Studios back when it was Westwood Associates before we became part of Virgin Games. Electronic Arts bought Westwood and even though they closed the studio they still own the rights to all Westwood’s stuff.

But none of that matter because it published by Strategic Simulations Inc
(SSI) which was bought out by Mindscape, became part of Mattel before finally ending up under Ubisoft.

It was actually developed under the AD&D license from TSR which was bought by Wizards of the Coast and is now owned by ummm Hasbro?

Along those lines, as you might know Westwood redid Dune 2 as Dune 2000 and then a follow-up with the same new engine Dune Emperor. This was about the same time Sci-Fi was having some success with the new Dune mini-series.

It was soon after that they said, "hey wait a minute! who are these guys publishing these Dune games?"

The original was done 10 years earlier under Virgin and now Westwood was owned by EA it seemed nobody bothered to do any legal follow-up to see if all the rights were in order and that is was okay to actually re-make Dune 2 and it's new sequel.

I have no idea how that story ends though as soon after my friend who was producer on those games left the industry and then Westwood was shut down. But it took 2 years plus a revitalized mini-series before somebody noticed there were video games based on that license.

So.... all in all I'll simply release my EOB-64 port in the Public Domain license since those big company dragons will never sort out who's really owns the EOB name.

Joseph was also most helpful and gave me a ZIP archive containg all the original .IFF files used for the games. I have this ZIP at work and it's about 10Mb. Could someone fix so that I can upload it here to The Zone!.

I asked Joseph about spreading this ZIP and his answer was: "What zip file? You found a zip file on the internet? I guess you can find anything if you look hard enough. I wonder where it came from. :-)".

So.

/Andreas
